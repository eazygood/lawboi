# Phase 3: Expansion

## Goal
Expand beyond the first accountant workflow while preserving legal reliability.

## Audience Expansion
- Startups
- In-house legal teams
- Broader professional services users
- Citizens using high-volume legal self-service workflows

## Domain Expansion
- Commercial law
- Consumer law
- Data protection
- Sector-specific regulatory domains
- Administrative procedure and fines
- Housing and property-related obligations

## Product Expansion
- Multi-workspace support
- Upload and analyze user documents against cited law
- Policy and contract review against statutory requirements
- Cross-linking to EU law where relevant
- Guided citizen journeys for procedural and rights-based questions

## System Enhancements
- Separate indexes for statutes, case law, and user documents
- Tenant-aware permissions and storage separation
- Deeper citation graph and legal topic taxonomy
- More advanced comparison and change-impact analysis

## Governance Rules
- Keep public law and private user documents distinct in retrieval
- Mark user-uploaded content as non-authoritative
- Continue statute-first answer generation
- Require source labels for every evidence type

## UX Additions
- Workspace dashboards
- Shared research folders
- Review workflows for teams
- Saved answer templates for recurring legal tasks
- Public-facing guided legal assistant experiences for repeat citizen questions

## Exit Criteria
- Multi-tenant support is stable
- New domains can be added with low operational overhead
- User-document analysis is grounded and clearly labeled
- Team workflows are supported without weakening trust features
